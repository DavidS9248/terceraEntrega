In logistics, a "carrier" refers to a company or entity that provides transportation services for the movement of goods, products, or cargo from one location to another. Carriers are an essential component of the supply chain and play a crucial role in ensuring the efficient and timely delivery of goods.


  • Trucking Carriers: These carriers use trucks, such as semi-trucks, to transport goods over the road. They can be further categorized into less-than-truckload (LTL) carriers for smaller shipments and full-truckload (FTL) carriers for larger, dedicated shipments.
  • Rail Carriers: These carriers operate trains for the transportation of goods over long distances. Rail transport is often used for bulk cargo, such as coal, grain, and raw materials.
  • Air Carriers: Air carriers, including commercial airlines and dedicated cargo airlines, transport goods by air. This mode of transportation is known for its speed, making it suitable for time-sensitive and high-value shipments.
